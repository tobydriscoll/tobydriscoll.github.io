Academic integrity
You are allowed and encouraged to discuss problems with others. However, you must never represent someone else's work as your own. Sharing or copying of work may result in grade penalties for all involved parties. You should carefully read the official university policies regarding academic integrity.
Homework
If I had eight hours to chop down a tree, I'd spend six sharpening my axe. --Abraham Lincoln

Homework will be assigned and graded using the webassign system. Assignments will generally be due weekly, on Sundays. The homework problems are crucial to your success in learning, as well as in preparation for the exams. While the online system is used to mark your results, your TA and instructor are available to help you understand the problems and solutions.
Quizzes
During each Tuesday discussion meeting you will be given a short quiz based on the problems in the most recently due homework set. The quizzes count for your grade and are good practice for answering questions on exams.
Discussion meetings
The will to win is important, but the will to prepare is vital. --Joe Paterno

The Tuesday and Thursday discussion meetings are considered problem-solving sessions during which you can learn from the TA and your peers. To get good use of this time, it is important for you to have attempted at least some of the currently assigned problems. Part of your grade is based on the TA's assessment of your participation in these meetings.
Exams
There will be three midterm exams on nonoverlapping units of the course, and a comprehensive final. No electronic devices will be allowed during the exams.
