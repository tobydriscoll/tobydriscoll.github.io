Synopsis

Partial differential equations typically result from models of phenomena that depend continuously on more than one variable---in most cases, one or more space dimensions and/or time. These equations arise frequently in virtually all fields of physical, biological, and earth science and engineering, as well as in certain social sciences such as economics. PDEs exhibit a wide variety of behaviors, even as compared to ordinary differential equations (one variable only), and understanding even one in detail can take a life's work. Fortunately, they can be grouped into three broad classes, each of which is represented by a major type of equation from classical physics. The majority of our time will be devoted to understanding the Big Three models, their major mathematical properties, and the tools used to explore them. We will also encounter more specialized problems and get some insight into their behavior.
