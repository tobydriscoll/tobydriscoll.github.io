You live in a heavily quantified world. Once in numerical form, data can be subjected to algorithms in order to yield insights. While the breadth and pace of these efforts have accelerated recently, the tradition of applying algorithms to numerical representations of reality goes back to the roots of civilization in virtually all major cultures. In the twentieth century, these efforts picked up steam as automatic and reprogrammable computers were invented. Soon there will be very few areas of scientific and economic activity left untouched by numerical algorithms.

Because the real numbers are an abstraction that we can only approximate, the mathematics of numerical computation is subtle at times. Mathematically equivalent formulas may be quite different in terms of a computer implementation, so multiple approaches to the same problem may be necessary. In fact, most of this semester will be spent on seemingly "elementary" algebra problems. Our motivation for these problems come from major areas of current research, such as image processing, machine learning, and molecular dynamics. While we only skim the surface of these huge subjects, applications of them and in other areas of computing very often boil down to the same kinds of algebraic problems, just as advanced mathematics often involves some algebraic manipulation along the way.

Here is an overview of the topics we will study:

Linear algebra MATLAB, matrix multiplication, identity, inverse
Floating point arithmetic Roundoff error, conditioning, stability
Linear systems of equations LU and PLU factorization, operation counting, norms, condition numbers
Linear least squares Normal equations, QR factorization, pseudoinverse
Nonlinear equations and least squares Newton and quasi-Newton methods, Gauss-Newton iterations, globalizing convergence
Eigenvalues Power iteration, inverse iteration, Schur factorization, singular value decomposition
Large scale problems (time permitting) Sparse matrices, matrix-free iteration, Krylov subspaces, GMRES, conjugate gradients
