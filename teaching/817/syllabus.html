Synopsis

Differential equations are the main language in which mathematical models of the world are expressed. Almost every differential equation that can be written down has no closed-form solution. Numerical approximations for the solutions of these equations are therefore indispensible to scientific computation.

Broadly speaking, there are two choices for these approximations: simulate derivatives, or simulate integrals. Each has pros and cons, but this course is mainly focused on the first of these strategies. We will describe and understand these methods as applied to both steady-state and time-dependent phenomena. On simple model problems we can do a great deal of rigorous analysis, while the methods themselves can be very effective for more complicated and realistic problems.
