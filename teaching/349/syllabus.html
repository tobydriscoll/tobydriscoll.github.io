Synopsis
MATH 349 is unique in that it combines a rigorous approach to mathematical material in a subject that is absolutely vital to every branch of the natural and quantitative social sciences. The central topic is linearity, which is an extension of the simple straight line to multiple variables. Many important phenomena are naturally linear, and in other cases linearity can usually serve as a good first approximation. Linearity also creates a very precise and well-defined sort of mathematical structure.
