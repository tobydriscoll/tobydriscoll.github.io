Synopsis
MATH 508 is about one of the most beautiful topics in mathematics: complex numbers. At first they may seem like an unlovable kludge--indeed, mathematicians disdained them for centuries. But in fact they lead to results that are deep and surprising, yet easily stated. Furthermore, we know now that complex numbers are sometimes the simplest way to unify or illuminate many problems in mathematics, physics, and engineering.
