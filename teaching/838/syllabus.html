Academic integrity
You are expected and encouraged to discuss problems with others. However, the work you turn in must be completely your own. For example, fixing an error in one line of a classmate's code is no big deal. Emailing your entire solution to someone is a big deal and is a violation of the integrity policy. So is working side-by-side and turning in the same code with some variable names changed.

Sharing or copying will result in grade penalties for all involved parties.

Homework
You will be given 3 homework assignments. Some problems will be purely analytical, but a majority will be computational. The homework will account for half of your part I grade.
MATLAB
I expect that you will use MATLAB for the homework. (If you have a compelling reason not to, come see me. I can't promise much help for programs written in other languages.) I have written a basic guide that should be enough to get you through the class. The online manuals with MATLAB and at mathworks.com are thorough and include tutorials. I will also be doing demos in class and making my codes available on the web.
Presentation
You will choose a published journal paper on RBFs from a list that I will furnish before the end of February. One of the final lecture periods in March will be given to you for presenting the material in the paper. You will be expected to demonstrate mastery of the material in the paper. This lecture will account for half of your part I grade.
