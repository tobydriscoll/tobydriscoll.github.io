Synopsis
Math 428 is about the translation of the basic tools of calculus to numerical computation. Since calculus is about functions and computers deal with finite lists of numbers, we first need to discuss the process of interpolation connecting the two. Then we show how to differentiate and integrate in the computational sense. From there we move on to solving differential equations, first ordinary (one independent variable) and then partial (in space and time, or multidimensional space). The subjects and techniques are connected perhaps a bit more directly than they are in exact analysis.
