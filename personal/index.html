Digital photography

My most enjoyable toy at present is my digital camera (an Olympus 2020). You can see some of the results at this site. I use Jasc's Paint Shop Pro software at home. (I used to like The GIMP a lot, but it crashed frequently for me on my home Windows machine. This was a few years ago, however.) Jasc also maintains a Digital Photography Center with useful tips; another interesting site is Retouch PRO. I recommend Snapfish for getting prints. The quality and durability are excellent, and the cost is comparable to, if not better than, ink and paper at home. If you're impatient to see a few prints, you can always go to a kiosk at a camera store or Wal-Mart. If you're chronically impatient, then by all means go buy a printer!

Sports & rec

Being a daddy and living in the East (instead of Colorado) has put a real damper on my ability to get outdoors a lot. Except for a brief go in the Poconos area of Pennsylvania, my snowshoes haven't seen much action the last few years.

One thing I've picked up since moving to Delaware is racquetball. Racquetball seems to have fallen out of fashion somewhat in the US, which is a shame. It combines elements of power, speed, coordination, and strategy, and it's a good workout. I'm an intermediate player at best, but I always have a good time playing.

I also have a brown belt in Isshinryu Karate, though I'm not currently working out in any dojo and practice only sporadically.
